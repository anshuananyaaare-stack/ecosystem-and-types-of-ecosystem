Ecosystem and Types of Ecosystem

A high-impact natural system where living organisms and non-living factors align together, creating balance, energy flow, and survival across the planet.

Start Learning

1) Meaning of Ecosystem

An ecosystem is a natural unit where living organisms (plants, animals, microorganisms) interact with non-living components (air, water, soil, sunlight) in a particular area.

These interactions create a stable environment by maintaining energy flow and nutrient cycling.

2) Components of Ecosystem

‚úÖ A) Biotic Components (Living)

  • Producers: Green plants and algae (photosynthesis)
  • Consumers: Animals depend on other organisms
  • Decomposers: Bacteria and fungi break down dead matter

‚úÖ B) Abiotic Components (Non-Living)

  • Sunlight
  • Air
  • Water
  • Soil and minerals
  • Temperature
  • Humidity

3) Types of Ecosystem

‚úÖ A) Natural Ecosystem

Natural ecosystems are formed by nature without human influence.

1) Terrestrial Ecosystem (Land Ecosystem)

  • Forest Ecosystem: High rainfall, large trees, rich biodiversity
  • Grassland Ecosystem: Grass dominates, grazing animals
  • Desert Ecosystem: Very little rainfall, cactus, camels
  • Tundra Ecosystem: Extremely cold climate, frozen soil, mosses
  • Mountain Ecosystem: High altitude, unique plants & animals

2) Aquatic Ecosystem (Water Ecosystem)

  • Freshwater Ecosystem: Rivers, ponds, lakes
  • Marine Ecosystem: Seas and oceans
  • Estuarine Ecosystem: River meets sea (mixed water)

‚úÖ B) Artificial Ecosystem

Artificial ecosystems are created and managed by humans.

  • Aquarium
  • Crop fields (Agricultural ecosystem)
  • Gardens and parks
  • Man-made ponds
